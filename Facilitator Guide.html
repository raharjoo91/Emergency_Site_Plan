Emergency Field Hospital Site Planning Exercise

Facilitator Guide – 45-Minute

Exercise Overview

Exercise NameField Hospital Site Planning Exercise
Duration45 minutes
Mission AreasResponse, Protection
Core CapabilitiesHealthcare Delivery, Logistics, Planning
Threat/HazardAll-hazards (earthquake, flood, volcanic eruption)

Objectives

  1. Design a compliant 2D site layout for an emergency field hospital based on a selected scenario (Type 1, 2, or 3).
  2. Apply minimum technical standards for functional zones, WASH, and safety buffers.
  3. Integrate essential services: command, reproductive health, and mental health (MHPSS) where required.

Site Planning Requirements

All layouts must include:

  1. Site boundary with dimensions
  2. Six functional zones: Clinical, Support, Logistics, Administration, Staff, Security
  3. Patient entry point (front)
  4. Ambulance bay
  5. Clinical tents with bed capacity
  6. Water tank (15) + purifier (14)
  7. Sanitation ≥30m from water
  8. Medical waste ≥500m from population
  9. Utility corridors
  10. Staff accommodation with separate WASH
  11. Emergency exits
  12. Parking/loading area
  13. Security perimeter with flag (19)

Remote earthquake, 80m × 60m, fully tented.

Urban flood + cholera, 120m × 100m, hybrid.
Scenario C → Type 3 (100–200+ beds)
Volcanic eruption, 150m × 120m, hybrid + isolation.

Exercise Structure (45 Minutes Total)

  • 0–10 min: Introduce scenarios; teams select one (A, B, or C)
  • 10–35 min: Teams draft 2D layout using grid paper or whiteboard
  • 35–45 min: Quick team share + facilitator-led discussion using hotwash questions

Discussion Questions

  1. Is Command/Admin (Tenda 1) included?
  2. Are patient and staff WASH facilities separated and ≥30m from water?
  3. Is reproductive health integrated (Type 2/3)?
  4. Is MHPSS included for Type 2/3?
  5. Are medical waste and genset placed safely?
